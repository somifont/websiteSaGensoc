All of the Anti-Trans Executive Orders Trump Has Signed

Althea Kabigting, Francis Aldave

During his time in office, particularly in early 2025, Trump signed Executive Order 14168, titled "Defending Women from Gender Ideology Extremism and Restoring Biological Truth to the Federal Government." This wasn't just a symbolic gesture; it laid out concrete directives with significant real-world implications.

What Do These Orders Mean?

At its core, this order mandated that federal agencies redefine gender strictly as an unchangeable male-female binary, determined at conception. It pushed for the replacement of "gender" with "sex" in official documents and aimed to halt federal funding for gender-affirming care and anything seen as promoting "gender ideology."

Prisons: Courts have already started issuing temporary restraining orders to block forced transfers and ensure access to gender-affirming care.

Passports and Official Documents: The order restricts self-selection of gender markers on federal documents like passports, sparking lawsuits alleging violations of due process and equal protection.

Information Access: Government health agencies were directed to remove web pages that promoted "gender ideology," though judges have ordered some of these pages to be restored, even with new disclaimers.

This isn't just about politics; it's about people, their identities, and their access to essential rights and care. The outcome of these challenges will undoubtedly shape the future of gender equality and personal freedom in America.

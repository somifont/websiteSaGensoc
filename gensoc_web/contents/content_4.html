Pope Francis Fields Questions from Students About Gender Identity and LGBTQ Discrimination

Pope Francis visits the Middle East. | Credit: Vatican Media

In a candid exchange with university students from across the Asia-Pacific, Pope Francis recently delved into the complex and often sensitive topics of gender identity and LGBTQ+ discrimination within the Church. His remarks offer a compelling starting point for dialogue on "gender and development in the Church," emphasizing empathy, proximity, and the vital role of women in leadership.

Inside the conversation: The pope underscored that "gender discrimination" can be overcome through "closeness and proximity." This highlights a call for greater understanding and accompaniment, rather than judgment, in addressing these issues. He also championed the importance of women, particularly their capacity for "motherhood" and their ability to foster community, suggesting their indispensable contribution to the Church's future.

“The Register is absolutely vital, and a major part of my educational, professional, personal, and faith life” —Matthew Bunson, Ph.D.

However, the discussion also brought to light the lived experiences of LGBTQ+ individuals facing expulsion and bullying, as shared by a student from the Philippines. Simultaneously, concerns were raised about the teaching of "gender theory" in Catholic schools, to which Pope Francis responded by stressing the importance of solid faith formation in Catholic education.

This insightful dialogue provides a rich foundation for exploring how the Church navigates contemporary understandings of gender and identity while upholding its teachings. It prompts us to consider how communities of faith can become spaces of greater welcome, understanding, and development for all.
